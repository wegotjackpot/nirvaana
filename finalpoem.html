you.

If I were a star,
and you were a star,
I’d blink at you, wink at you, twinkle at you —
and the world would call it science. But I would know,
it’s us. I adore the texture of your mind,
how your thoughts unfold like constellations,
quiet, infinite, and full of wonder. The perfume of your soul stays in the room,
like rain on parched earth,
soft, stubborn, unforgettable. Your face is my chosen abyss.
I could fall into it a thousand times
and never want to be found. Your lips taste like honey,
your eyes burn like wildfire,
and I, like a fool,
keep walking into the flame. Looking at you makes me forget
how to exist in the world properly.
You turn me into this clumsy, wide-eyed child —
counting the seconds between your glances,
hoarding your smiles like treasure,
memorizing the shape of your laugh. when you are yourself,
I watch like it’s art.
When you laugh your heart out —
I forget what sadness feels like. When I catch you off guard,
and your ears flush before your cheeks,
my heart writes novels in the span of a heartbeat. On days the world is too loud,
too sharp,
too much,
I look at you. And you become the silence between songs,
the warmth before the rain,
the pause in a perfect sentence. you become the gentle patter of raindrops on a window,
you are the rain-soaked evening,
wrapping me in its quiet,
in its cool embrace. and you become the hope that lives
between today and all my tomorrows. and if the sky ever forgets
how to hold stars,
or the rain forgets how to fall, i will still remember
how you made my world feel
like poetry — and I will keep loving you
in the spaces
where words run out. In silence,
in stardust,
in everything.
